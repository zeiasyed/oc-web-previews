Before you decide

Understand your options

Leasing and retail finance (buying) both get you into a Honda — but they work very differently. This guide explains the concepts, then asks a few questions to recommend what may fit you best.

Leasing

Rent the vehicle for a fixed term

You pay for the vehicle's depreciation plus a finance charge over a set period — typically 36 months on Honda Financial Services (HFS) leases.

  • You do not own the car unless you buy it at lease-end
  • Lower monthly payment than financing the full price
  • Mileage limits apply (often ~10,000 miles/year on HFS)
  • Return the vehicle or purchase at a preset residual value

Retail Finance (Buying)

Borrow to own the vehicle

You take a loan for the vehicle price and pay interest (APR) over a term — commonly 36 to 72 months through HFS or another lender.

  • You build equity and own the car once the loan is paid off
  • Higher monthly payment, but no mileage caps
  • Freedom to sell, trade, or keep the vehicle as long as you want
  • Payment-free years possible after the loan is satisfied

Key differences at a glance

Factor Lease Retail Finance
Ownership Lender owns; you return or buy out You own after loan payoff
Monthly payment Usually lower Usually higher
Mileage Limited; overage fees apply No limits
Best timeline 2–3 years, then new vehicle 5+ years of ownership
End of term Turn in, lease again, or buy Keep driving with no payment
